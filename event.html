Most people treat ChatGPT like Google. That’s a mistake. You need to treat it like a brilliant but literal intern. If you ask Google a vague question, it guesses standard results. If you ask an intern a vague question, they deliver the completely wrong project. Engineering a system means precisely defining the context, the tone, and the exact constraints of what you expect.

The C.R.E.A.T.E. Framework

A systematic approach to zero-shot prompting.

C • Context

Market, stage, and background.

A • Audience

Who is reading this output?

R • Role

The expert persona to simulate.

T • Tone

Professional, visionary, etc.

E • Explicit

Clear, unambiguous instructions.

E • Evaluation

Required format and length.

This framework is your core blueprint. Before hitting 'Enter', check if your prompt hits these 6 marks. For example, don't just ask for a "marketing plan". Set the CONTEXT of your Seed-stage FinTech. Assign the ROLE of a Growth Lead. Target Gen-Z AUDIENCE. Use a persuasive TONE, and ask for EVALUATION output as a markdown table. Another example: If you need SEO research, set the context of your specific niche, assign the role of an 'SEO Expert', and explicitly instruct it to output a JSON list of long-tail keywords.

Logic &
Reasoning

"Let's think step-by-step."

This single phrase reduces hallucination by 40-60% in complex tasks.

Chain-of-Thought prompting is the secret to unlocking reliable business logic from LLMs. By forcing the model to explicitly state its reasoning process before delivering the final answer, you force it to stay within the bounds of reality. For example, if you ask an LLM to calculate Customer Acquisition Cost based on raw ad spend data and user signups, it might hallucinate the final number. But if you append "Let's think step-by-step: first list total spend, then count acquired users, then divide," the accuracy leaps massively. It allocates 'compute time' for the model to think before it concludes.
