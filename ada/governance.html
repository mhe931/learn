AI Governance & Ethics

EU AI Act

The world's first comprehensive AI law classifies systems by risk:

  • Unacceptable Risk: Banned. (Social Scoring, Real-time Biometrics).
  • High Risk: Critical infrastructure, Hiring, Credit Scoring. Requires strict conformity assessments.
  • Limited Risk: Chatbots. Requires transparency ("I am an AI").
  • Minimal Risk: Spam filters, Games. No obligations.

NIST AI Risk Management Framework (RMF)

A voluntary US framework based on 4 functions:

  1. GOVERN: Cultivate a risk culture.
  2. MAP: Identify context and risks.
  3. MEASURE: Quantitative analysis of risks.
  4. MANAGE: Prioritize and treat risks.

The Certification Debate: ISO vs Traficom

The battle for trust labels:

  • ISO 42001: The Global Standard for AI Management Systems. Enterprise-grade, rigorous, expensive.
  • Traficom Label: The Finnish Approach. A voluntary traffic-light label ensuring basic transparency and security.
EXAM DEBATE: Should companies seek global ISO certification (Hard) or local labels (Easy)?
Answer: Global enterprises need ISO 42001 for compliance. Local startups might prefer Traficom for agility.
