AI Governance & Ethics

EU AI Act

The world's first comprehensive AI law. It classifies AI systems by risk:

  • Unacceptable Risk: Banned (e.g., social scoring, real-time biometric ID).
  • High Risk: Critical infrastructure, employment, credit scoring. Requires strict conformity assessments.
  • Limited Risk: Chatbots, emotion recognition. Requires transparency (labeling).
  • Minimal Risk: Spam filters, video games. No obligations.

NIST AI Risk Management Framework (RMF)

A voluntary framework to better manage risks to individuals, organizations, and society.

GOVERN

Culture of risk management.

MAP

Context and risks identified.

MEASURE

Analyze and track risks.

MANAGE

Prioritize and treat risks.

IBM AI Risk Atlas

A taxonomy of risks associated with AI, including:

  • Input/Output Risks: Hallucinations, Poisoning.
  • Model Risks: Bias, Drift, Overfitting.
  • System Risks: Security, Privacy, Compute usage.
