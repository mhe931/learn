AI & Agentic Systems

The Evolution of GenAI

From static models to dynamic agents, the landscape is shifting rapidly:

  • LLM (Large Language Model): The foundation. Static knowledge up to training cutoff.
  • RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation): Injecting context. Connecting LLMs to vector databases for private data access.
  • Graph RAG: Structured context. Using Knowledge Graphs to provide semantic relationships to the LLM, reducing hallucinations.
  • Agentic AI: Systems that can reason, plan, and execute actions. Agents don't just answer; they DO.

Model Context Protocol (MCP)

A standardized way to provide context to models. Instead of messy prompts, MCP defines a schema for how models perceive their environment, tools, and memory. It enables interoperability between different AI agents and tools.

Multi-Agent Systems (MAS)

Orchestrating specialized agents to solve complex problems. Patterns include:

  • Hierarchical: A "Manager" agent delegates tasks to worker agents.
  • Sequential: Output of one agent becomes input for the next (Chain of Thought).
  • Joint Collaboration: Agents debate and refine answers (e.g., a "Coder" and a "Reviewer").
