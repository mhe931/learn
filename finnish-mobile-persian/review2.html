Partitive Case (Partitiivi)

Partitive Formation (Partitiivin muodostaminen)

The partitive ending is added to the word stem based on the final vowel(s):

  • Single vowel $-e$: $-tta/-ttä$ (e.g., osoite → kolme osoitetta)[span_10](end_span).
    • [span_11](start_span)
  • Another single vowel: $-a/\ddot{a}$ (e.g., talo → kaksi taloa)[span_11](end_span).
    • [span_12](start_span)
  • Two vowels: $-ta/-tä$ (e.g., huone → kaksi huonetta)[span_12](end_span).
    • [span_13](start_span)
  • Consonant: $-a/\ddot{a}$ (e.g., englanti → Puhun englantia.)[span_13](end_span).
[span_14](start_span)

Personal Pronouns in Partitive: minua (mua), sinua (sua), häntä (sitä), meitä, teitä, heitä (niitä)[span_14](end_span).

When to use the Partitive (Milloin käytät partitiivia)

[span_15](start_span)

The partitive is often used when other languages use the plural form, but it is not the same as the plural[span_15](end_span).

    [span_16](start_span)
  1. After **numbers** (except for one/yksi): viisi kirjaa (five books)[span_16](end_span).
    2. [span_17](start_span)
  2. After **quantifiers**: monta kirjaa (many books)[span_17](end_span).
    3. [span_18](start_span)
  3. With **substance words** (uncountable): kahvia, mehua, leipää, pizzaa, ilmaa, rahaa[span_18](end_span).
    4. [span_19](start_span)
  4. With **negative sentences**: Minulla ei ole autoa. (I don't have a car.)[span_19](end_span).
    5. [span_20](start_span)
  5. With **irresultative phrases** (action is incomplete or ongoing): Luen kirjaa. (I am reading a book.)[span_20](end_span).
  6. With **partitive verbs**: e.g., rakastaa (to love), odottaa (to wait for), pelata (to play), ajaa (to drive), opiskella (to study), auttaa (to help). [span_21](start_span)Example: Poika rakastaa tyttöä. (The boy loves the girl.)[span_21](end_span).

Note: All words in a phrase (e.g., a noun phrase) will be put in the partitive when required. [span_22](start_span)Example: Ajan sinistä autoa. (I drive a blue car.)[span_22](end_span).

Verb Types (Verbityypit)

[span_23](start_span)

Remember consonant gradation (astevaihtelu)[span_23](end_span).

  • Type 1: Vowel + $-a/\ddot{a}$. [span_24](start_span)E.g., asua (to live), puhua (to speak), maksaa (to cost, to pay)[span_24](end_span).
  • Type 2: $-da/-dä$. [span_25](start_span)E.g., käydä (to visit, to go), voida (can, to feel), syödä (to eat)[span_25](end_span).
  • Type 3: $-lla/\ddot{l}\ddot{a}$, $-nna/\ddot{n}\ddot{n}\ddot{a}$, $-rra/\ddot{r}\ddot{r}\ddot{a}$, $-sta/\ddot{s}\ddot{t}\ddot{a}$. [span_26](start_span)E.g., tulla (to come), mennä (to go), nousta (to get up, to rise)[span_26](end_span).
  • Type 4: $-ata/\ddot{a}\ddot{t}\ddot{a}$, $-ota/\ddot{o}\ddot{t}\ddot{a}$, $-uta/\ddot{y}\ddot{t}\ddot{a}$. [span_27](start_span)E.g., osata (can, to be able to), avata (to open), siivota (to clean)[span_27](end_span).
  • Type 5: $-ita/\ddot{i}\ddot{t}\ddot{a}$. [span_28](start_span)E.g., tarvita (to need), häiritä (to disturb), valita (to choose)[span_28](end_span).

I-words (I-sanat)

[span_54](start_span)

Inflection differs between 'new' (loanwords like kahvi, teksti) and 'old' (nature words like kivi, saari) I-words[span_54](end_span).

    [span_55](start_span)[span_56](start_span)
  • New I-words: Inflect as usual (e.g., Partitive: basic form $+a/\ddot{a}$, Genitive: basic form $+n$)[span_55](end_span)[span_56](end_span).
  • Old I-words: Go through more changes. Partitive: remove $-i$ and add $-ea/\ddot{e}\ddot{a}$ or $-ta/\ddot{t}\ddot{a}$. [span_57](start_span)Genitive: remove $-i$ and add $-en$[span_57](end_span).
      [span_58](start_span)
    • E.g., joki (river) $\rightarrow$ Partitive: jokea, Genitive: joen[span_58](end_span).
      • [span_59](start_span)
    • E.g., lapsi (child) $\rightarrow$ Partitive: lasta, Genitive: lapsen[span_59](end_span).

Imperative (Imperatiivi)

[span_60](start_span)

Used in orders and requests, expressing necessity or wishes[span_60](end_span).

    [span_61](start_span)
  • Formation: Drop the personal ending $-n$ from the $1^{st}$ person singular present tense (e.g., Syödä $\rightarrow$ Syön $\rightarrow$ Syö!)[span_61](end_span).
    • [span_62](start_span)
  • Negative form: $\ddot{A}l\ddot{a}$ + imperative (e.g., $\ddot{A}l\ddot{a}$ syö! - Don't eat!)[span_62](end_span).
    • [span_63](start_span)
  • Examples of Use: order (Ole hiljaa! - Be quiet!), prohibition ($\ddot{A}l\ddot{a}$ aja ylinopeutta! - Don't speed!), wish (Voi hyvin! - Take care!)[span_63](end_span).

Possession and Existence Sentences

Possession (Omistuslause)

[span_64](start_span)

Expresses that someone has or does not have something (Someone has/doesn't have something)[span_64](end_span).

Structure: (Adessive case of person) + (to be, olla) + (Subject in Nominative/Partitive).

    [span_65](start_span)
  • Affirmative: Tytöllä on hevonen. (The girl has a horse.)[span_65](end_span).
    • [span_66](start_span)
  • Negative: Hänellä ei ole koiraa. (She does not have a dog.) (Object is in Partitive)[span_66](end_span).

Existential Sentences (Eksistentiaalilause)

[span_67](start_span)

Expresses that something is somewhere ("There is" or "There are")[span_67](end_span).

Structure: (Location case) + (to be, olla) + (Subject in Nominative/Partitive). [span_68](start_span)Sentences start with a location case[span_68](end_span).

    [span_69](start_span)
  • Example: Huoneessa on sänky. (In the room is a bed. / There is a bed in the room.)[span_69](end_span).
    • [span_70](start_span)
  • Example: Pöydällä on kirja. (On the table is a book. / There is a book on the table.)[span_70](end_span).

Persian Verb Types 1 and 2 (Added Content)

In Persian, verb classification is less about endings and more about the structure of the **verb stem** (present and past) and the rules for forming tenses. This is an analogy to the Finnish system.

فعل نوع ۱: افعال با قاعده (Regular Verbs - Analogous to Finnish Type 1)

این افعال از یک ریشه گذشته و یک ریشه حال برای ساختن زمان‌های مختلف استفاده می‌کنند.

  • ساختار: ریشه گذشته از حذف مصدر (ن-یدن/ن-فتن) و ریشه حال که اغلب یکسان است.
  • مثال: رفتن (Raftan - To Go)
    • ریشه گذشته: رفت (raft)
    • ریشه حال: رو (row)
  • زمان حال (Present Tense): می + ریشه حال + شناسه (Mi + Row + Shanaaseh)
    • من می‌روم (Man miravam - I go)
    • تو می‌روی (To miravi - You go)

فعل نوع ۲: افعال بی‌قاعده (Irregular Verbs - Analogous to Finnish Type 2)

این افعال ریشه گذشته و ریشه حال کاملاً متفاوتی دارند و باید حفظ شوند.

  • ساختار: ریشه گذشته و حال از نظر ظاهری ارتباط مستقیمی ندارند.
  • مثال: دیدن (Didan - To See)
    • ریشه گذشته: دید (did)
    • ریشه حال: بین (bin)
  • زمان حال (Present Tense): می + ریشه حال + شناسه (Mi + Bin + Shanaaseh)
    • من می‌بینم (Man mibinam - I see)
    • تو می‌بینی (To mibini - You see)
